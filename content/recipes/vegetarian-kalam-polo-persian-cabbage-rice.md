date: 2026-05-29T11:47:00.000Z
emoji: "🍽️"
dietaryTags: "Veg, Vegan, Gluten-Free, Dairy-Free"
ingredients:
  - name: "Cabbage"
    amount: "3 cups (1-inch cubes)"
  - name: "Onion"
    amount: "1 medium"
  - name: "Dry soy chunks (or ground beef)"
    amount: "1½ cups"
  - name: "Rice"
    amount: "2 cups"
  - name: "Turmeric"
    amount: "1 tsp"
  - name: "Cumin"
    amount: "1 tsp"
  - name: "Paprika"
    amount: "1 tsp"
  - name: "Cinnamon"
    amount: "2–3 tbsp"
  - name: "Salt and Pepper"
    amount: "To taste"
  - name: "Olive oil (or neutral vegetable oil)"
    amount: "as needed"
  - name: "Butter (optional)"
    amount: "4, 1 inch cubes"
  - name: "Bloomed saffron (optional)"
    amount: "1 tbsp"
steps:
  - title: ""
    body: "Finely chop the onion. Heat oil in a pan and sauté until slightly golden."
  - title: ""
    body: "Soak soy chunks in water for 5–10 minutes. Drain, then add to the onions. Add turmeric, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook for a few minutes until lightly browned."
  - title: ""
    body: "Add cabbage, mix well, and cook with the lid on, on medium heat for about 10 minutes until slightly softened. Set aside."
  - title: ""
    body: "Wash rice until water runs clear. Add to a pot with 5 cups water, 1 tsp salt, and 1 tbsp oil. Bring to a boil, then cook on medium heat for 5-10 minutes until slightly tender (do not fully cook). Strain."
  - title: ""
    body: "Heat a pot with oil. Add saffron if using. Layer: a thin layer of rice, then cabbage mixture, a sprinkle of cinnamon, and small cubes of butter (optional). Repeat until all ingredients are used."
  - title: ""
    body: "Cover and cook on medium heat for 25–30 minutes until rice is fully cooked and fluffy."
  - title: ""
    body: "For crispy tahdig, increase heat to medium-high for the last few minutes. Remove from heat, flip onto a serving plate, and serve."
howToServe:
  - "Best served fresh but can be store in fridge for up to 3 days."
  - "If prepping ahead for hosting, you can prep and layer the rice and filling, cook later just before serving."
chefsNote: "Substitute soy with ground beef is desirable. (follow the same cooking steps) Remove butter or use vegan butter for dairy free and vegan. Great for meal prep. Cook just before serving."
