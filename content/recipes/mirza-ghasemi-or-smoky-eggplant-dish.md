dietaryTags: "Veg, Gluten-Free, Dairy-Free"
ingredients:
  - name: "Eggplants"
    amount: "Medium or 2 large"
  - name: "Tomatoes"
    amount: "4–5 medium"
  - name: "Tomato paste"
    amount: "2 tbsp"
  - name: "Garlic"
    amount: "1 head"
  - name: "Eggs"
    amount: "4"
  - name: "Neutral oil"
    amount: "As needed"
  - name: "Salt and Pepper"
    amount: "To taste"
steps:
  - title: ""
    body: "Grill the eggplants on a charcoal barbecue if possible (gas barbecue works too) until the skin is slightly burnt and the inside is completely soft. Remove from heat and let cool for about 10 minutes."
  - title: ""
    body: "In the meantime, finely chop or crush the garlic. Fry in oil until fragrant."
  - title: ""
    body: "Chop the tomatoes and add to the garlic. Cook on high heat for about 10–15 minutes until softened and thickened into a sauce-like texture."
  - title: ""
    body: "Add the tomato paste and mix well."
  - title: ""
    body: "Peel the grilled eggplants and chop until paste-like. Add to the tomato mixture. Season with salt and pepper and mix everything together."
  - title: ""
    body: "Add the eggs. You can stir them in for a quicker cook time, or leave them whole for presentation."
  - title: ""
    body: "Cover with a lid and cook for another 5–10 minutes until the eggs are cooked but the yolks remain slightly runny."
howToServe:
  - "Serve with fresh herbs, yogurt, or a fresh salad on the side."
  - "Can be served as a main dish with rice, or as an appetizer with some flat bread."
chefsNote: "Best served fresh, but can be stored in the fridge for up to 2 days. If using garlic powder instead of fresh garlic, use generously. Great for meal prep, add the eggs just before serving for best texture."
