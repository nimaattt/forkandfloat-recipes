date: 2026-05-29T11:53:00.000Z
emoji: "🍽️"
dietaryTags: "Veg, Vegan, Dairy-Free"
ingredients:
  - name: "Zucchini"
    amount: "1 large"
  - name: "Chickpeas"
    amount: "1 can"
  - name: "Fresh Cilantro"
    amount: "1 cup"
  - name: "Olive oil"
    amount: "2 tbsp"
  - name: "All purpose Flour"
    amount: "1/2 cup"
  - name: "Cornstarch"
    amount: "1 tbsp"
  - name: "Frying oil"
    amount: "1 cup"
  - name: "Salt and Pepper"
    amount: "to taste"
  - name: "Lemon"
    amount: "1"
  - name: "Garlic Powder"
    amount: "1 tsp"
  - name: "Cumin Powder"
    amount: "2 tsp"
steps:
  - title: ""
    body: "Prep zucchini"
  - title: ""
    body: "Grate the zucchini on the fine side of your grater. Place in a cheesecloth or clean kitchen towel, and squeeze out as much moisture as possible. Set aside."
  - title: ""
    body: "Blend base"
  - title: ""
    body: "In a food processor, pulse chickpeas, cilantro, olive oil, salt, and pepper until it forms a coarse paste (don’t over-blend)."
  - title: ""
    body: "Combine"
  - title: ""
    body: "Transfer mixture to a bowl, add the grated zucchini, cumin, garlic powder, juice of a lemon, flour, and cornstarch. Mix well until it holds together. If the mix feels too wet, add a little extra flour."
  - title: ""
    body: "Shape"
  - title: ""
    body: "Form small rounds, patties, or your preferred falafel shape."
  - title: ""
    body: "Cook"
  - title: ""
    body: "Heat a thin layer of olive oil(or your frying oil of choice)  in a skillet over medium heat. Pan-fry falafels in batches, turning to cook each side until golden and crisp."
  - title: ""
    body: "Serve"
  - title: ""
    body: "Enjoy warm with tzatziki, in bowls, or as part of a mezze spread."
howToServe:
  - "Bowls: serve over quinoa, brown rice or fresh greens, layered with cucumbers, tomatoes, radishes and pickled onions. Drizzle with tahini sauce or tzatziki."
  - "Wrap: Tuck felafels into a warm pita or flatbread, add your vegetables of choice and sauce it will tzatziki or tahini sauce."
  - "Serve felafels as small bites on a platter."
chefsNote: "Make sure the zucchini is well-drained. Excess water will make the mixture fall apart. You don’t need to deep fry; a shallow pan-fry keeps them light yet crispy. These reheat well in the oven the next day. Use chickpea flour if Gluten-Free."
