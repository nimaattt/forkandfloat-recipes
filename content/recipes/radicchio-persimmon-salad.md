ingredients:
  - name: "Radicchio"
    amount: "1 head"
  - name: "Persimmon"
    amount: "1"
  - name: "Pomegranate molasses"
    amount: "1 tbsp"
  - name: "Balsamic Vinegar"
    amount: "1 tbsp"
  - name: "Olive oil"
    amount: "1/4 cup"
  - name: "Honey"
    amount: "1 tsp"
  - name: "Nutritional Yeast"
    amount: "1 tbsp"
  - name: "Grainy Mustard"
    amount: "1 tbsp"
  - name: "Roasted Salted Pistachios"
    amount: "1/4 cup"
  - name: "Salt and Pepper"
    amount: ""
steps:
  - title: ""
    body: "Prep the radicchio:"
  - title: ""
    body: "Cut the radicchio into large wedges. Drizzle with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast at 350°F for 6–7 minutes, just until slightly softened and edges begin to char."
  - title: ""
    body: "Slice the persimmon:"
  - title: ""
    body: "Cut into thin rounds or wedges (whatever feels prettiest on the plate)."
  - title: ""
    body: "Make the dressing:"
  - title: ""
    body: "Whisk together pomegranate molasses, balsamic, olive oil, honey, nutritional yeast, salt, and pepper until smooth and glossy."
  - title: ""
    body: "Assemble:"
  - title: ""
    body: "Arrange the roasted radicchio and fresh persimmon on a plate. Drizzle generously with the dressing."
  - title: ""
    body: "Finish:"
  - title: ""
    body: "Top with crumbled goat cheese, chopped salty pistachios, and a few dollops of grainy mustard."
howToServe:
  - "Plate on a shallow bowl or wide plate so the colors and textures stay visible."
  - "Replace goat cheese with Vegan cheese if Vegan or Dairy Free."
  - "Best served fresh but can keep in the fridge for 2 days. Make sure to add the dressing and the toppings right before serving."
  - "Add the pistachios right before serving to keep them crunchy."
chefsNote: "Radicchio is rich in antioxidants and vitamin K, and roasting softens its bitterness. Add a handful of arugula if you want more greens without changing the mood of the dish. If your persimmon isn’t fully ripe, slice it thinner, it blends better with the warm radicchio."
