title: "Salmon Tahdig"
description: "A crispy Persian-style herbed rice layered over salmon with garlic, butter, and saffron."
category: "Mains"
tags:
  - mains
  - persian
prepTime: "10 mins"
cookTime: "35 mins"
servings: "4"
difficulty: "Easy"
coverImage: "/images/uploads/salmon-tahdig.png"
date: 2026-05-29T11:55:00.000Z
emoji: "🍽️"
dietaryTags: "Dairy-Free, Contains Shellfish"
ingredients:
  - name: "Salmon"
    amount: "6–7 pieces"
  - name: "Rice"
    amount: "2 cups"
  - name: "Dill"
    amount: "⅓ cup"
  - name: "Cilantro"
    amount: "⅓ cup"
  - name: "Parsley"
    amount: "⅓ cup"
  - name: "Chives"
    amount: "⅓ cup"
  - name: "Garlic"
    amount: "4 cloves"
  - name: "Oil"
    amount: "1 tbsp"
  - name: "Butter"
    amount: "4–5 small cubes"
  - name: "Bloomed saffron (optional)"
    amount: "2 tbsp"
  - name: "Salt and Pepper"
    amount: "To taste"
steps:
  - title: ""
    body: "Wash the rice several times until the water runs clear."
  - title: ""
    body: "Add the rice to a medium pot with the herbs, 2 tsp salt, and 1 tbsp oil. Cover with water and bring to a boil."
  - title: ""
    body: "Let boil for about 4 minutes, then remove from heat and strain. The rice should still be slightly undercooked."
  - title: ""
    body: "In the same pot, heat enough oil to cover the bottom."
  - title: ""
    body: "Add the salmon pieces to the pot. If they are very thick, slice them in half horizontally first. Season with salt and pepper and add the garlic cloves on top."
  - title: ""
    body: "Add half of the herbed rice on top of the salmon. Dot with butter and saffron."
  - title: ""
    body: "Add the remaining rice and top again with butter and saffron."
  - title: ""
    body: "Cover and cook on medium heat for about 30 minutes until the rice is fluffy and the bottom forms a crispy tahdig."
  - title: ""
    body: "Carefully flip onto a serving platter before serving."
howToServe: []
chefsNote: "Saffron is optional but adds traditional flavor and color. For crispier tahdig, increase the heat slightly during the last few minutes of cooking. Not great for meal prep. Best served fresh."
