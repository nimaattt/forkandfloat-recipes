ingredients:
  - name: "Persimmon"
    amount: "2 medium (super ripe)"
  - name: "Egg"
    amount: "1"
  - name: "Sugar"
    amount: "1/4 cup"
  - name: "Cinnamon Powder"
    amount: "1 tbsp"
  - name: "All Purpose Flour"
    amount: "1 cup"
  - name: "Baking Powder"
    amount: "1 tbsp"
  - name: "Butter"
    amount: "1 tbsp"
  - name: "Icing Sugar (for garnish)"
    amount: "1 tbsp"
  - name: "Pecans (for garnish)"
    amount: "1.4 cup"
steps:
  - title: "Prep"
    body: ""
  - title: ""
    body: "Cut 2 medium, super ripe persimmons in half and scoop out the flesh into a bowl. Discard the skins."
  - title: ""
    body: "Puree the persimmon flesh fully until smooth (blender or immersion blender)."
  - title: ""
    body: "Add 1 egg and sugar to the puree. Mix well until smooth and combined."
  - title: ""
    body: "Add cinnamon and then start adding flour, mixing as you go."
  - title: ""
    body: "You want a fairly thick batter (thicker than a crepe, closer to pancake batter)."
  - title: ""
    body: "If it’s runny, add a bit more flour and mix again."
  - title: ""
    body: "Keep adding in small amounts until it holds its shape when spooned."
  - title: "Cook"
    body: ""
  - title: ""
    body: "Heat a flat pan on medium heat. Lightly brush with butter."
  - title: ""
    body: "Spoon batter onto the pan to form pancakes."
  - title: ""
    body: "Cook 3–4 minutes on the first side (until bubbles form and edges look set), then flip and cook another 2–3 minutes until golden and cooked through."
  - title: "Serve"
    body: ""
  - title: ""
    body: "Top with icing sugar or maple syrup, and finish with crushed pecans."
howToServe:
  - "Serve warm, straight from the pan for the best texture."
  - "Pairs beautifully with coffee or hot tea for a winter breakfast or brunch."
chefsNote: "Use very ripe persimmons (almost jammy). If they’re even slightly firm, the batter won’t be as sweet or smooth. A thick batter is key. It should slowly fall off the spoon, not pour. If the batter looks too loose, add flour 1 tablespoon at a time. Cook on medium heat; persimmons have natural sugars and can burn if the heat is too high. Flip only once the edges look set and the bottom is lightly golden (about 3–4 minutes). These pancakes are naturally sweet, so taste before adding syrup — you may not need it. Best enjoyed fresh, but leftovers can be reheated gently in a pan."
